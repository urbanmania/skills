# Research checklist

A checklist for stage 2 of the persona-creator workflow. The goal is to make sure the demographic context is genuinely understood before personas are drafted, not approximated from generic global assumptions.

Not every dimension is relevant to every persona. Use judgement. But scan the full list — a dimension you skipped because it "obviously doesn't matter" is often the one that ends up mattering most.

---

## Persona methodology

- [ ] What does good practice say about personas for this kind of product? (See `persona_best_practice.md`)
- [ ] Are there domain-specific persona conventions? (Healthcare personas, accessibility personas, B2B personas have different norms.)
- [ ] If for user testing: what recruitment criteria will the personas need to support?
- [ ] If for accessibility review: which accessibility dimensions need to be embedded in the persona itself?

## Product domain

- [ ] What is the typical user journey in this product area?
- [ ] What are common pain points users encounter in this domain?
- [ ] What regulatory or compliance context applies? (Especially for finance, healthcare, child-facing products.)
- [ ] What competing products exist? What are users currently using?
- [ ] What domain vocabulary will users encounter? Is it jargon-heavy?

## Demographic — life stage

- [ ] What developmental, life-stage, or career-stage characteristics define this group?
- [ ] What cognitive, physical, or emotional factors are typical at this stage?
- [ ] How does this stage affect technology use? Decision-making? Risk tolerance?

## Demographic — geography and culture

- [ ] What are the cultural norms around the product domain? (Money culture, health culture, privacy culture vary enormously by country.)
- [ ] What are family or household norms in the location?
- [ ] What naming conventions apply? (Patronymics, family-name-first, religious naming, etc.)
- [ ] What language(s) does this group speak? Which is dominant for emotional and trust-related communication?
- [ ] What is the urban/rural mix? Does the persona set need to represent both?
- [ ] What socioeconomic range exists within this demographic? Avoid defaulting to the most affluent slice.

## Demographic — daily context

- [ ] What does a typical day look like for this group? Where are the time pressures?
- [ ] Where and when do they have private, focused time? Where do they make decisions?
- [ ] What institutions (school, workplace, healthcare, government) are part of their daily life?
- [ ] Who else is in their daily life? Family, peers, colleagues, carers — who influences them?

## Demographic — digital context

- [ ] What is typical device access for this group? Personal, shared, family-controlled?
- [ ] What apps and platforms dominate their digital life?
- [ ] What is their comfort with financial / health / professional / governmental digital tools specifically?
- [ ] What national-level digital habits are documented? (Many countries have national surveys on screen time, internet use, etc.)
- [ ] What digital safety frameworks apply? (Especially for minors and vulnerable groups.)

## Demographic — financial context (if relevant)

- [ ] What is the local currency and the typical price points for goods relevant to this group?
- [ ] How is this group typically introduced to money? (Allowances, jobs, benefits, family transfers.)
- [ ] What financial institutions are present in their lives? What is the cultural relationship with banks?
- [ ] What is the typical level of financial literacy at this life stage?
- [ ] Is the local economy cash-heavy or cashless? This matters for how digital money feels.

## Demographic — health and accessibility context (if relevant)

- [ ] What are the prevalence rates of relevant conditions in this demographic?
- [ ] What is the local healthcare system structure? Public, private, mixed?
- [ ] What is the cultural relationship with clinicians, mental health, disclosure?
- [ ] What accessibility needs are common at this life stage and need representation in the persona set?

## Demographic — trust and safety context

- [ ] What are the documented safety risks for this group in this product domain?
- [ ] What are the typical sources of distrust? (Scam exposure, institutional history, language barriers.)
- [ ] What regulatory protections apply specifically to this group?
- [ ] For minors: guardian consent, parental controls, child safety frameworks.
- [ ] For vulnerable adults: capacity, advocacy, safeguarding considerations.

## Source quality

Prefer in this order:

1. **Government and official statistics** — national censuses, health authorities, central banks, ministries of education, regulatory bodies.
2. **Academic research** — peer-reviewed studies, university research centres, established research organisations.
3. **National surveys** — established annual or biennial surveys of the demographic in question.
4. **Authoritative news and policy organisations** — reputable journalism, established think tanks.
5. **Reputable practitioner sources** — professional bodies, established consultancies in the domain.

Be cautious of:

- Marketing content presented as research
- AI-generated articles that compound stereotypes
- Single-source generalisations
- Sources from very different cultural contexts being applied to the target demographic
- Older sources for fast-changing domains (digital habits more than five years old are often outdated)

## When research gaps exist

If, after searching, a specific dimension cannot be confirmed (for example, no national survey exists on children's allowances in a particular country), name the gap explicitly in the persona document. Mark the relevant claim as an assumption. Do not paper over it with confident-sounding prose.

A clearly labelled gap is honest. A papered-over gap is a future bug.
